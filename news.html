Bitcoin Surpasses $75K Amid Institutional Inflows

May 13, 2025 • By Sarah Blockchain

After months of sideways movement, Bitcoin has finally broken the $75,000 barrier, signaling renewed confidence among institutional investors.

Bitcoin rallied 8.2% over the past 48 hours, driven largely by capital inflows from newly approved ETFs and increased demand from Asia. Analysts attribute this surge to a combination of macroeconomic easing, the weakening dollar, and regulatory clarity from the SEC regarding crypto funds.

Why the Momentum Might Continue

According to data from Glassnode, long-term holders are accumulating again. The recent uptick in wallet activity suggests that both retail and institutions are preparing for a larger bull run. Additionally, BlackRock's updated ETF application, now including staking derivatives, has excited the market.

Many experts believe this run could reach new all-time highs by Q3. However, volatility remains high and investors are advised to remain cautious in the face of ongoing macro uncertainty.

Ethereum Fees Drop to $0.03 After Dencun Upgrade

May 12, 2025 • By Mark DeFi

The long-awaited Dencun hard fork is live — and it's already delivering results. Ethereum gas fees are down over 90% across most L2s.

Layer 2s like Optimism, Base, and zkSync have all reported record transaction volumes following the upgrade. “Blob” transactions (EIP-4844) are now standard, allowing cheaper data posting on-chain while retaining Ethereum’s security guarantees.

What This Means for the Ethereum Ecosystem

Lower fees drastically improve user experience and dApp viability. Combined with increased throughput and faster finality, Dencun may serve as the catalyst for mass Layer 2 adoption. Developers are already migrating legacy apps to blob-compatible rollups.

Meanwhile, Vitalik Buterin emphasized that this is just the beginning of Ethereum’s roadmap toward scalability — the next step: "The Verge."
